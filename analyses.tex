\subsection{Description of
visualization}\label{description-of-visualization}

The plot shows the evolution of the height and weight of male and female
Olympic Athletics sporters and compares them between 1928 and 2016. It
is a 4-way facet grid each layered with two scatterplots, a regression
line and a density plot. One scatter plot is for that years' data points
(little bit see-through circles) and the other is for the full amount of
data points of both years (light grey 'x's).

The top half is dedicated to women athletes and the bottom half is for
the male athletes. Since 1928 was the first year in which women could
participate for athletics, the data size is quite small. However, you
can clearly see that in 1928 they are all clustered around a short
height and small weight while by 2016 the data cluster shifted to taller
heights and bigger weights; thus indicating a strong positive
relationship. The same goes for the male athletes, albeit less prevalent
but still quite a noticeable difference.

Overall, you can see that over the decades athletes have become bigger
in both ways, which indicates changes in the sport itself, but also in
the way of training and nutrition.

Shortcomings might be that it solely focuses on 1928 and 2016 and
doesn't really show the evolution over all the years inbetween. Lastly,
I'm just focusing on athletics and leaving out all the other sports.
These require completely different body types and it would be
interesting to see their history.
